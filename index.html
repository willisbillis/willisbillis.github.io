About Me

I'm a bioinformatics analyst currently working between the Boss-Scharer and Kohlmeier labs at Emory University's Department of Microbiology and Immunology, specializing in single-cell bioinformatics, systems biology, and graph theory applications.

My path to bioinformatics began in the cosmos. I earned my Bachelor of Science in Astrophysics from The University of Georgia in 2020, where I spent my undergraduate years studying computational fluid dynamics of interstellar hydrogen clouds that fuel star formation in the Milky Way under Robin Shelton's mentorship.
